The University of California, San Diego (UCSD) is a public research university in La Jolla, California: Location The main campus is located on 1,200 acres of coastal woodland near the Pacific Ocean. History Founded in 1960, UCSD is one of the ten campuses of the University of California. Academics UCSD offers over 200 undergraduate and graduate degree programs, including the Scripps Institution of Oceanography and the UC San Diego Health System. Rankings UCSD is recognized as one of the top 20 research universities in the world. It's also ranked first in the nation for research, civic engagement, and social mobility. Student life UCSD has over 600 student organizations, and 1 in 3 undergraduates study abroad. Health system The UC San Diego Health System includes more than 1,500 doctors and scientists, including 50 members of the National Academy of Medicine. Campus life UCSD has performance venues, fitness classes, and other ways to find community and have fun.
